# Use Cases

AtlasConvert serves a wide range of users across multiple domains. Here are the primary use cases.

## Academic & Research

### Historical Economic Analysis

**Problem**: Researchers need accurate historical purchasing power data for economic analysis.

**Solution**: AtlasConvert provides academically-sourced purchasing power estimates for 15+ historical coins.

**Example**:
> A researcher studying Roman trade routes needs to compare the value of a Denarius across different centuries. AtlasConvert provides decade-by-decade purchasing power data with source citations.

**Benefit**: Accurate, cited data for academic publications and research papers.

### Metrology Studies

**Problem**: Historians encounter unfamiliar measurement units in historical documents.

**Solution**: AtlasConvert's Measurement Decoder mode identifies and converts archaic units to modern equivalents.

**Example**:
> A historian reading a 16th-century Ottoman tax record encounters "50 dirhems of silver." The Measurement Decoder converts this to 160.35 grams, with context about Ottoman weight standards.

**Benefit**: Instant understanding of historical documents without manual research.

## Creative Writing & Publishing

### Historical Fiction

**Problem**: Novelists need accurate period economics for believable stories.

**Solution**: AtlasConvert provides context-aware conversions with narrative context.

**Example**:
> A writer setting a novel in 18th-century France needs to know what a Louis d'Or could buy. AtlasConvert shows it could buy a fine meal at a Parisian restaurant, equivalent to about $500 today.

**Benefit**: Authentic period details that enhance historical fiction.

### Game Development

**Problem**: Game designers need realistic historical pricing for in-game economies.

**Solution**: AtlasConvert's Market Simulator estimates historical commodity prices.

**Example**:
> A game developer creating a medieval RPG needs to price a suit of armor. The Market Simulator shows a full suit cost about 10-15 Louis d'Or, roughly $5,000-$7,500 in modern terms.

**Benefit**: Historically accurate game economies that enhance immersion.

## Education

### Classroom Learning

**Problem**: Teachers need engaging tools to teach measurement history.

**Solution**: AtlasConvert's interactive Museum and Map make learning visual and engaging.

**Example**:
> A history teacher shows students the Atlas Map, tracing the geographic spread of Roman currency. Students click on coins to see their purchasing power and discuss economic influence.

**Benefit**: Interactive learning that makes history tangible.

### Curriculum Integration

**Problem**: Educators need resources that align with history and math curricula.

**Solution**: AtlasConvert covers both measurement systems and their historical contexts.

**Example**:
> A math teacher uses AtlasConvert to teach unit conversion while incorporating historical context: "If a Roman merchant bought 10 jugerum of land, how many square meters is that?"

**Benefit**: Cross-disciplinary learning that connects math and history.
